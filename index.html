How it works

The IgniteGrapple workflow for your room

The beta keeps things simple: your phone, your room, your film. We help you organize, tag, and turn it into action.

1

Capture what matters.

Film live goes, situationals, or matches on your phone. No fancy camera rig required — just get the room in view.

2

Upload & tag in the beta app.

Use the IgniteGrapple dashboard to tag athletes and practice type. The AI layer (rolling out over beta) helps identify patterns and problem spots.

3

Turn it into a weekly plan.

Build practice themes, live-go ladders, and focus lists based on actual film — not guesses. Lock in what each group is working on.

4

Share progress the right way.

Create coach-only views or athlete/parent-friendly snapshots so everyone understands the direction of the room.

During beta, IgniteGrapple runs as a web app — so if you can open a website, you can use the system. As we learn from rooms like Future Champs of Dallas, we’ll expand into deeper AI tools and mobile apps.

Founder & partner room

Built by someone who actually lived the grind.

Kevin Almond, MBA – Founder of IgniteGrapple

IgniteGrapple is built by Kevin Almond — a wrestler who came from a low-income background, no father figure, and learned stance and motion using rug patterns and yard space before ever stepping onto a mat.

Kevin started wrestling in 8th grade on JV, then climbed all the way to the Georgia state finals from 10th–12th grade, winning a state title as a senior. In college, he became the first NCAA All-American in Emmanuel College history, and the first All-American across all sports when the school joined Division II in 2018.

Along the way he earned his MBA in Human Resource Management, became a single father, and learned how real life pressure hits off the mat too. IgniteGrapple is his way of giving coaches a smarter system so they can give their kids the chances he had to fight for.

“I know what it feels like to be the underdog room — small budget, no extra help, but big expectations. IgniteGrapple exists so those rooms can think, plan, and develop like they have a whole staff behind them.”
